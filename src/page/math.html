A cipher is an algorithm for performing encryption or decryption‚Äîa series of well-defined steps that can be followed to encrypt or decrypt a message.

The Caesar cipher shifts each letter in the plaintext by a fixed number of positions down the alphabet. For example, with a shift of 3, A becomes D, B becomes E, and so on.

RSA is a public-key cryptosystem that uses two large prime numbers to generate a public and private key pair. It's based on the mathematical difficulty of factoring large composite numbers. The public key encrypts messages, while only the corresponding private key can decrypt them.
